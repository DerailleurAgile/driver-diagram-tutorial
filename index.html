Driver Diagram Tutorial

What it is and how to use it

A Driver Diagram is a structured map of your theory of improvement. It shows what you believe needs to change in a system, and why, in a form that a whole team can see, challenge, and test.

It is not a plan. It is a set of hypotheses. Every arrow is a claim: if we change this, we expect that to improve. The diagram makes those claims explicit so they can be examined — and so that PDSA cycles can test them.

Deming connection: In The New Economics, Dr. Deming teaches that "Theory is a window into the world. Theory leads to prediction. Without prediction, experience and examples teach nothing." A Driver Diagram is a way to make your predictions about an improvement visible and testable, with the benefit of creating knowledge.
